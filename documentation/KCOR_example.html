KCOR Worked Example — 5 Weeks with Detrending from Last 2 Weeks

Setup

  • Cohort: Birth year 1940; comparison: Dose 2 vs Dose 0.
  • Weeks: 5 consecutive weeks; assume equal person-time per arm each week (PT = 1000).
  • We’ll compute the detrend baseline as the mean of the last two weeks’ log-ratios (a flat anchor over the last 2 weeks).
  • Weights ww = PT (equal here), so the weighted mean reduces to a simple average.

Detrending baseline from the last 2 weeks

Take the last two weeks’ log-ratios and average them (flat anchor over weeks 4–5):

  • log(Ratio)4 = 0.251314
  • log(Ratio)5 = 0.117783
  • Baseline = (0.251314 + 0.117783) / 2 = 0.184549

KCOR with detrending (point estimate)

With equal PT, the weights are equal, so the cumulative mean of detrended log-ratios through week 5 is the simple average:

μ(5) = (Σ ỹ) / 5 = (0.151924 + 0.103133 - 0.051017 + 0.066766 - 0.066766) / 5
     = 0.040808

KCOR(5) = exp( μ(5) ) = exp(0.040808) = 1.041652

Interpretation: after removing the baseline drift (anchored on the last two weeks), the cumulative relative risk over 5 weeks is ~4.2% higher for Dose 2 vs Dose 0.

For equal weights ww=1, the variance of the mean μ over 5 weeks is:

Var[μ] = (Σ Var_w) / 5^2 = 0.0556984
SE = sqrt(Var[μ]) = 0.236005

95% CI on the log scale: μ ± 1.96·SE ⇒ 0.040808 ± 1.96·0.236005

Exponentiate to ratio scale:

KCOR_LCL = exp(0.040808 - 1.96*0.236005) = 0.655890
KCOR_UCL = exp(0.040808 + 1.96*0.236005) = 1.654300

These CIs are wide because weekly deaths are small in this toy example. With real data and larger counts, CIs will tighten.

Notes

  • The “detrend baseline” here is a flat level = mean of the last two log-ratios (weeks 4–5). You could also fit a slope over those weeks (not identifiable with only 2 points), or use a longer anchor window (e.g., calendar 2023).
  • With unequal PT, set weekly weight ww = min(PTw,treat, PTw,ref) and use the weighted formulas for μ and Var[μ].
  • If you turn detrending off (baseline = 0), the KCOR equals the ratio of cumulative CMRs.
