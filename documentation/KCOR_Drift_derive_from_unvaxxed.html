Adjusted Deaths/Week Derivation

This guide outlines how we adjusted weekly deaths in vaccinated and unvaccinated cohorts to remove population-level drift. By correcting for baseline mortality trends, we can isolate true differences in outcomes attributable to the intervention (e.g., vaccination).

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Trim to Post-HVE Window
    Start analysis after enrollment week + HVE skip period.
  2. Measure Unvaccinated Slope
    • Apply an 8-week moving average to unvax deaths/week.
    • Extract strict local minima (lowest in Â±2-week window and lower than previous minima).
    • Fit exponential decay:
      \( D(t) = b \cdot e^{a_u t} \)
      Result: \( a_u = -0.00213 \) with \( R^2 \approx 0.977 \)
  3. Estimate Pre-Vax Population Slope
    • Use pre-vax baseline (e.g., Aprâ€“Aug 2020).
    • Observed slope is roughly flat: \( a_1 \approx 0 \)
  4. Compute Vaccinated Slope Indirectly
    Using conservation of population deaths:

    \[ a_3 = \ln\left( \frac{A \cdot e^{a_1} - B \cdot e^{a_2}}{A - B} \right) \]

    • \( A = 2000 \), total deaths/week
    • \( B = 900 \), unvax deaths/week
    • \( a_1 = 0 \), flat pre-vax trend
    • \( a_2 = -0.00213 \), decay for unvax
    Result: \( a_3 \approx 0.001739 \)
  5. Normalize Raw Deaths
    • Unvax: \( D_{\text{adj}}(t) = D(t) \cdot e^{-a_2 t} \)
    • Vax: \( D_{\text{adj}}(t) = D(t) \cdot e^{-a_3 t} \)
