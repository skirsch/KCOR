KCOR Method with Drift Normalization

The Kirsch Cumulative Outcome Ratio (KCOR) method evaluates all-cause mortality between intervention and control groups (e.g., vaccinated vs. unvaccinated), accounting for selection effects and demographic drift. This document provides a full description of the KCOR methodology as applied to the Czech all-ages dataset, incorporating our new exponential slope-based drift normalization.

1. Background

Traditional observational comparisons suffer from confounding due to non-proportional hazards (e.g., vaccinated cohorts being healthier). KCOR improves this by standardizing death rates within well-defined observation windows.

3. Adjusted Death Normalization

Drift in background mortality is normalized using exponential correction of deaths per week for each cohort. The method is detailed in the linked document: Adjusted Deaths/Week Derivation

Unvaxxed slope \( a_2 = -0.00213 \), derived from local minima of smoothed data. Vaccinated slope \( a_3 = 0.001739 \) was computed using:

\[ a_3 = \ln\left( \frac{A \cdot e^{a_1} - B \cdot e^{a_2}}{A - B} \right) \]

4. KCOR Computation Steps

  1. Aggregate deaths per week by cohort as of enrollment date.
  2. Apply drift normalization:
    • Unvax: \( D_u(t) \cdot e^{-a_2 t} \)
    • Vax: \( D_v(t) \cdot e^{-a_3 t} \)
  3. Cumulate adjusted deaths over time to get \( C_u(t), C_v(t) \)
  4. Compute KCOR(t): \( \frac{C_v(t)}{C_u(t)} \)
  5. Normalize KCOR(t) using baseline period to form final flat-asymptote ratio

5. Results

After correction, KCOR(t) stabilizes. A final value greater than 1 indicates higher all-cause mortality in the vaccinated cohort.

Example: If final KCOR(t) = 1.20, this implies a 20% excess all-cause mortality in the vaccinated cohort over the observation window.

6. Visual Sanity Checks

  • Unvax Adjusted Curve: Should be flat across full observation window
  • Vax Adjusted Curve: Should be flat after vaccine-induced effects stabilize
  • KCOR(t): Should level off and not rise indefinitely in a drift-neutral scenario

7. Notes

Because the unvaxxed cohort is often higher-risk, failing to adjust for drift can lead to overestimation of vaccine benefit. KCOR controls for this by using slope-normalized comparisons based on population-level constraints.
